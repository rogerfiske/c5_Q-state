## 1. Executive Summary

This project analyzed 34 years of data (February 1992 -- April 2026) from five parallel discrete time-series variables (QS_1 through QS_5) to discover underlying patterns and generate next-day probabilistic forecasts using TabICLv2.

**Key Finding**: The data represents **sorted order statistics from repeated draws of 5 numbers without replacement on {1..39}**. The process is temporally i.i.d. -- each draw is completely independent of all previous draws. There is no trend, no seasonality, and no exploitable autocorrelation across 34 years of history.

**Deliverable**: Top-5 most likely values with calibrated probabilities for each QS_x on April 9, 2026, generated by TabICLv2 Classifier trained on the full 11,762-observation history.

## 3. Phase 1: Exploratory Data Analysis

### 3.1 Key Discoveries

1. **Order-statistic structure**: QS_1 through QS_5 are the 5 sorted values from draws on {1..39}. QS_1 is always the smallest, QS_5 always the largest. Each variable occupies a distinct band of the value space with no overlap at the extremes.

2. **Mirror-image distributions**: QS_1 piles up at the floor (mode=1, right-skewed), QS_5 piles up at the ceiling (mode=39, left-skewed), and QS_3 sits at the symmetric center (skew nearly zero). This is the classic beta-distribution shape expected from order statistics of a discrete uniform.

3. **Tridiagonal correlation pattern**: Adjacent variables share strong mutual information due to the ordering constraint (if QS_2 is high, QS_3 must be higher). Distant pairs (QS_1/QS_5) are weakly correlated.

4. **Complete temporal stationarity**: Rolling 30-observation means show no drift, regime changes, or structural breaks across 34 years. The underlying process has been constant since 1992.

5. **Near-daily with systematic gaps**: 96% of observations are consecutive days. The 340 two-day gaps and 125 four-day gaps follow a systematic pattern (likely non-draw days).

## 5. Conclusions

### 5.1 What We Learned About the Underlying Process

1. **The data is a lottery-type draw**: 5 numbers drawn without replacement from {1, 2, ..., 39}, then sorted in ascending order. This is the only model that simultaneously explains the discrete integer values, the {1..39} range, the order-statistic distributions, the tridiagonal correlation structure, and the temporal independence.

2. **The process is memoryless**: 34 years of data show zero practical autocorrelation. Each draw is independent. No amount of feature engineering or sophisticated time-series modeling can outperform the marginal distribution as a predictor.

3. **TabICLv2 correctly recovers the marginal**: The model's predictions closely match historical frequencies, confirming it learned the true distribution rather than overfitting to spurious lag patterns. The slight probability sharpening (~1% more weight on mode values) is attributable to the model's softmax temperature.

4. **Predictability varies by position**: The extreme positions (QS_1, QS_5) are the most predictable -- their top-5 covers >53% of probability mass. The middle position (QS_3) is near-uniform and essentially unpredictable beyond its range constraint.

### 5.2 Model Performance Assessment

TabICLv2 performed well given the nature of the data:
- Correctly identified the i.i.d. structure (did not overfit to lag features)
- Recovered accurate marginal distributions from 10-lag supervised framing
- Produced calibrated probabilities close to empirical frequencies
- Ran in under 5 minutes on CPU for all 5 models

### 5.3 Limitations

- **No joint prediction**: Each QS_x was modeled independently. A joint model respecting the ordering constraint (QS_1 < QS_2 < ... < QS_5) and the without-replacement constraint could produce valid 5-tuples.
- **CPU-only inference**: GPU acceleration would enable larger context windows and ensemble sizes.
- **The "prediction" is fundamentally the distribution**: For an i.i.d. process, the best forecast is the marginal distribution itself. No model can do better.

## 6. Recommendations for Future Work

1. **Joint combinatorial model**: Model the draw as selecting 5 from 39 directly, producing valid 5-tuples with proper joint probabilities. This would enforce the ordering and without-replacement constraints.

2. **Conditional analysis**: Investigate whether the draw mechanism has changed over time by comparing distributions across decades (e.g., 1992--2000 vs 2020--2026).

3. **Gap pattern analysis**: The 340 two-day and 125 four-day gaps may encode the draw schedule. Modeling the schedule could predict *when* the next draw occurs.

4. **Expected value calculations**: Given the probability distributions, compute expected returns for any downstream application that depends on these draws.

5. **Validation**: Compare predictions against actual April 9, 2026 draw results when available.
